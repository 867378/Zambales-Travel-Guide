FEAST OF INA POON BATO

The Feast day of Ina Poonbato, celebrated annually on January 24th, is a deeply revered and solemn occasion, particularly in Botolan, Zambales, where her shrine stands as a beacon of faith and hope. This day holds profound significance for devotees who gather from far and wide, drawn by their unwavering belief in her miraculous intercession and healing powers.
As the sun rises on this auspicious day, pilgrims embark on a spiritual journey to the sacred shrine of Ina Poonbato, carrying with them the weight of their prayers and the burden of their illnesses. The air is thick with anticipation and reverence as they offer heartfelt supplications, seeking solace, comfort, and divine intervention in their time of need.
In the hallowed grounds of the shrine, amidst the gentle rustle of leaves and the soft whispers of prayers, pilgrims lay bare their deepest pains and afflictions, entrusting them to the benevolent spirit of Ina Poonbato. With unwavering faith and fervent devotion, they beseech her for healing and guidance, believing wholeheartedly in the power of her divine grace to alleviate their suffering and restore their health.
Throughout the day, the shrine becomes a sanctuary of faith and healing, as devotees come together in solidarity, united by their shared devotion to Ina Poonbato. Amidst the solemn rituals and sacred ceremonies, there is a palpable sense of hope and renewal, as the faithful draw strength from the enduring legacy of miracles attributed to her compassionate intercession.
As the sun sets on this sacred day of devotion, pilgrims depart from the shrine, their hearts uplifted and their spirits rejuvenated by the profound experience of communion with the divine. Though their journey may have been long and arduous, they leave with a renewed sense of faith and gratitude, knowing that in the presence of Ina Poonbato, miracles are not only possible but inevitable.
